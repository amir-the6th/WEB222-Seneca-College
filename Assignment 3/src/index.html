Overview

The International Space Station (ISS) is a spacecraft, a laboratory, and more importantly, a multi-nation construction project. It is like a luxurious home in the space; up to 10 astronauts are hosted in ISS at any one time. This so-called home is an advanced scientific laboratory that NASA is using in order to learn more about living and working in space. A variety of scientific experiments are being conducted on the International Space Station every day.

Construction

To construct this station, sixteen countries contributed: The United States, Russia, Canada, Japan, Belgium, Brazil, Denmark, France, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, and the United Kingdom.

The ISS was launched 21 years ago, on 20 November of 1998 and has been inhabited since November second, 2000. It circles the Earth in low Earth orbit, that means it flies 250 miles above Earth's surface.

Although the main construction of the station ended in 2011, it is continuously evolving to include new missions.

Statistics

The effort that was put into this project by these 16 countries resulted in a soccer field-sized spacecraft that is 109 meters long and 73 meters wide. Not forget to mention, the ISS is the largest man-made object in the universe. With the speed of 7.66 km/s, the ISS can orbit the entire plane every 90 minutes.

Cost

It is worthy to note that the ISS is the most expensive thing that humankind has ever made; the construction of this station costed over $150 billion.

Facts

Some quick, fun, and interesting facts about the ISS:

  1. There are only two bathrooms on the entire football field-sized station
    • The crewmembers filter back their urine and also the laboratory animals' into the drinking water supply
  2. Normally, space shouldn't smell like anything; though, the ISS, according to an ISS astronaut, smells like metallic-ionization, and therefore, is considered the first place that you can actually smell space in
  3. The ISS is the third brightest object in the night sky after the moon and Venus. I will explain this more in the next sections
  4. In order to stay in-shape while on-board the ISS, the astronauts must work out at least for two hours per day to maintain the standards of Earth-based living
  5. It's funny to know how astronauts tackle the daily tasks:
    • Crewmembers float across the station when dining. they don't have any dining table or chair; they have got to stabilize themselves and eat slowly so the food wouldn't float and run away
    • All the food is packaged, dehydrated, or canned so it doesn't need refrigeration and it is also easier to maintain and manage

How to see the ISS

Some may think that the ISS cannot be seen without telescope; the truth is, it is one of the easiest objects to spot in the night sky. It looks like a fast-moving airplane when you see it.

The space station is visible because it reflects the light of the Sun – the same reason we can see the Moon NASA
There are many websites and applications that you can get notified on when you can spot the ISS with unaided eyes. NASA’s Spot the Station website is a service that texts you when the ISS will over your location.
